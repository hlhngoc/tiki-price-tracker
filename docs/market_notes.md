## 0. Corporate Context (Tiki, company-wide)

### 0a. Financial distress & investor exit
- VNG's Q4/2024 financial report shows VNG withdrew all personnel from Tiki and reclassified Tiki Global from "associate company" to "long-term financial investment" — meaning VNG remains a major shareholder but no longer participates in operations or governance.
- As of 31/12/2024, VNG had invested 510 billion VND into Tiki Global; the value of this investment had already been written down to zero since Q1/2019 — VNG has recognized a full loss on the investment.
- Founder Trần Ngọc Thái Sơn stepped down as CEO in 2023 to focus on an IPO effort; as of the most recent reporting, that IPO has still not materialized.
- Per Tech in Asia, Tiki's FY2022 total revenue fell 7% YoY to below $200M USD, with a reported loss of $93M USD (up 39% YoY).

**Source tier:** Verified — Markettimes.vn / CafeF (14/02/2025), corroborated by a separate syndication on DNSE (identical content, same original source). Two independent re-publications of the same underlying report increase confidence this is not a one-off or fabricated claim.

### 0b. Platform-wide market share time-series
- Pre-TikTok Shop (Metric.vn, data window Nov 2021–May 2022): Shopee ~73%, Lazada ~20%, Tiki ~5.8%, Sendo ~1.4%.
- H1/2024 (Metric.vn): Shopee 67.9%, TikTok Shop 23.2%, Lazada 7.6%, Tiki ~1.3% (997.06 billion VND revenue).
- 2025 (per Section 1b below): Tiki + Lazada combined ~3%, Tiki alone estimated ~1%.
- YouNet ECI, Q2/2024: Tiki had only 8,800 active sellers (down 19.1% YoY), versus Shopee's 261,000.

**⚠️ Scope warning:** All figures in this subsection (5.8% → 1.3% → ~1%) are **platform-wide market share across all product categories** — not specific to books. This is a different metric from Section 1a's book-category-specific 3% figure. Do not cite the 0b time-series as evidence of book-category trends; it is company-wide context only, useful for understanding Tiki's overall trajectory and the pressure it's under, not as a direct stand-in for book-specific dynamics.

**Source tier:** Verified — Metric.vn / YouNet ECI, as reported by Markettimes.vn (14/02/2025).

## 1. Market Players & Share

### 1a. Book category specifically (Metric.vn e-commerce book report)
- **Shopee** ranks #1: 55.6% of revenue, 60.3% of units sold — driven by heavy subsidies and a large base of 3P sellers.
- **TikTok Shop** ranks #2: 40.6% of revenue, 36.9% of units — fueled by livestream and book-review content.
- **Tiki** ranks #3: only 3.0% of revenue, 2.1% of units.
- **Lazada** ranks #4: 0.7% of revenue, 0.6% of units.

**Source tier:** Third-party analyst (Metric.vn, `metric.vn/ereport/nganh-hang-sach`) — Verified.

### 1b. Overall Vietnam e-commerce market (all categories, not book-specific)
Per Metric.vn's 2025 e-commerce report (via VnExpress): Shopee leads with 56%+ market share (growth slowing), TikTok Shop has grown to 41%+ (closing the gap fast), while Lazada + Tiki combined shrank from 6% to 3% year-over-year. Total GMV across the 4 platforms reached ~429.7 trillion VND (~$16B), up ~35% YoY. Seller count declined ~7.5% to 601,800, attributed to stricter enforcement against counterfeit/low-quality goods. Shopee + TikTok Shop together now account for ~8% of Vietnam's *total retail* sector (up from 6.5% in 2024).

**Source tier:** Verified — Metric.vn 2025 report, cited via VnExpress (May 2026).

**⚠️ Important distinction:** Section 1a (3.0%) is Tiki's share of the *book category specifically*. Section 1b (3% combined with Lazada) is Tiki's share of *all product categories* on the 4 major platforms. These are different metrics pointing in the same direction (Tiki losing ground) but should not be conflated as the same number — one is book-specific, the other is platform-wide.

**Regulatory context:** Vietnam's new E-Commerce Law (passed Dec 10, effective July 1, 2026) requires seller identity verification via VNeID, aimed at reducing counterfeit goods and improving tax compliance. This is directly relevant to the counterfeit book problem discussed in Section 2 — a potential future shift in competitive dynamics.

## 2. Book Pricing Mechanics in Vietnam

### 2a. Publisher wholesale discount
Publishers typically offer wholesale discounts of 30–50% off cover price to online retailers/agents, scaling with committed order volume. Some public reporting on the 2022–2023 Tiki–publisher platform fee dispute (Znews/VnExpress coverage) cites a narrower range of 40–45%.

**Source tier:** Unverified / general industry claim — no specific publisher agreement or article was directly read and confirmed for this project. Treat as directional, not a confirmed fact.

### 2b. Estimated net margin (consultant estimate — derived calculation)
Starting from a ~40% publisher discount off cover price:
- Shop must offer a 20–25% customer-facing discount to stay competitive on Shopee/Lazada → gross margin drops to 15–20%.
- From there, deduct mandatory platform costs: payment fee (~3–4%), platform fixed fee (~2–5%), Freeship Xtra/Voucher Xtra participation (~5–7%), plus packaging, and return/damage costs.
- **Net result: ~2–5% net margin on cover price** — forcing a volume-driven survival strategy for 3P sellers.

**Source tier:** Consultant estimate — author's own cost-structure derivation, not a published figure. Assumptions (discount %, fee %) are estimates, not verified against any specific seller's actual P&L.

**2026 update — margins likely thinner:** A verified May 2026 article (Doanh Nghiệp Hội Nhập, 21/05/2026) confirms that Lazada, Shopee, and TikTok Shop all raised seller-facing fees in May 2026: Lazada's transaction fee rose from 5% to 6% plus a new fixed per-order infrastructure fee; Shopee raised Voucher Xtra fees from ~4% to 5.5% and added a new mandatory 1% ad-account contribution; TikTok Shop raised commission fees to 9–17.8% depending on category. This suggests the 2–5% net margin estimate above may now be even thinner than when the underlying assumptions were formed.

**Source tier:** Verified — Doanh Nghiệp Hội Nhập, 21/05/2026.

### 2c. Counterfeit book undercutting
Unofficial/counterfeit sellers reportedly discount books 50–70% off cover price (vs. 20–30% max for legitimate copies), enabled by low-cost printing and no royalty payments to publishers.

**Source tier:** Unverified / general industry claim — no specific news article was directly read and confirmed. If verified later (e.g., via a specific VTV/Tuổi Trẻ/Thanh Niên report), upgrade this source tier accordingly.

## 3. Tiki Trading (1P) vs. Marketplace (3P)

| Criteria | Tiki Trading (1P) | Marketplace (3P) |
|---|---|---|
| **Nature** | Tiki buys wholesale directly from publishers, warehouses and distributes itself. | Independent bookstores, agents, small shops, or publishers themselves open storefronts and sell directly on Tiki. |
| **Authenticity & quality** | 100% authentic guarantee; 111% refund policy if counterfeit is found; strict inbound QC. | Mixed — includes trusted "Mall" shops and small/unverified shops; higher risk of counterfeit or low-quality books if buyers don't vet sellers. |
| **Pricing mechanism** | Stable discounts (15–30%), rarely "race to the bottom," backed by large negotiated publisher discounts; often bundled with book-care/wrapping service. | Highly competitive on price — 3P shops will cut margin to the minimum, clear inventory, or undercut aggressively to win search ranking. |
| **Delivery speed** | Very fast (TikiNOW) — stock already in Tiki's own warehouse; 2-hour or same-day delivery. | Depends on the individual shop's own fulfillment speed and handover to the carrier. |
| **Assortment breadth** | Curated — focused on best-sellers and titles from major publishers (Nhã Nam, Trẻ, Kim Đồng, etc.). | Much broader — includes used books, rare books, specialized/academic texts that Tiki Trading doesn't carry. |

**Strategic shift:** Per YouNet ECI market research, Tiki previously pushed to expand its 3P marketplace to compete on catalog breadth against Shopee/Lazada. It has since pivoted: Tiki Trading (1P) now accounts for ~75% of Tiki's **total platform-wide GMV** (all categories, not book-specific — same scope caveat as Section 1). No book-category-specific 1P/3P split is currently available; whether the book category mirrors this platform-wide figure is an open question, not a confirmed fact (see Section 4).

**Why the pivot toward 1P:**
- **Protecting the core brand:** Books are central to Tiki's brand identity; unchecked 3P growth previously exposed Tiki to counterfeit-book scandals that damaged trust with its loyal customer base. Consolidating around 1P restores full quality control.
- **Leveraging logistics as a moat:** Book buyers often want to read immediately (new releases, skill books). Owning warehousing (1P) lets Tiki defend its fast-delivery advantage, which independently-run 3P shops struggle to match.

**Source tier:** Third-party analyst (YouNet ECI) for the GMV/strategic-shift claims; author's synthesis for the "why" reasoning (labeled as such, not attributed to YouNet directly).

## 4. Reconciling the Apparent Contradiction

Metric's data (Section 1) measures Tiki's market share against external competitors (Shopee, TikTok Shop) specifically within the online book category — by this measure, Tiki is losing ground to outside rivals in books. YouNet's data (Section 3), on the other hand, measures the GMV split between 1P and 3P *within Tiki itself, across all categories platform-wide* — by this measure, Tiki's internal revenue mix (company-wide) is shifting toward 1P.

These two metrics differ in scope on two dimensions at once — industry-wide book share vs. internal platform-wide revenue composition — so they are not contradictory, but they also cannot be combined into a single "book-category 1P story" without an additional assumption. Whether Tiki's *book-specific* 1P/3P mix follows the same ~75% pattern as the platform-wide figure is unverified and is treated as an open hypothesis (not a fact) for the Day 7–8 issue tree, not asserted here.

## 5. Note on Revised Engagement Question

The original draft engagement question (see guideline v3) assumed Tiki "leads" Vietnam's online book market. Market data (Metric.vn) shows Tiki holds only ~3% revenue share in books, ranking #3 behind Shopee and TikTok Shop. This premise has been revised:

> **Revised question:** "Books are Tiki's core category, but the platform holds only a small share (~3%) of Vietnam's online book market, which is dominated by Shopee and TikTok Shop. How should Tiki use pricing data from its Books category to strengthen its position — and what capability (people, process, governance) does it need to build to do this at scale?"

This reframing keeps "books as core/flagship category" (a strategic/brand claim, still valid) while dropping the unsupported "market leadership" claim (a share claim, not supported by data).
